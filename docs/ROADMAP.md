# Roadmap

Mirante4D is moving from a capable research prototype to a maintainable,
open-source scientific application.

## Now

- Prepare a sanitized MIT-licensed public source repository.

## Foundation

- Simplify ownership boundaries and runtime APIs.
- Replace dataset, identity, project-store, import, and rendering foundations
  through hard cutovers.
- Use sharded storage and bounded physical-object counts for large datasets.
- Qualify Linux/Vulkan behavior on named datasets and hardware up to 1080p.
- Replace duplicated tests with focused correctness, E2E, GPU, performance,
  and product-open evidence.

## Open Source

- Keep contribution policy lightweight and maintainer-led.
- Use free public CI only; keep trusted GPU/display checks local.
- Publish truthful pre-alpha documentation and reproducible small fixtures.

## Later

- Publish selected microscopy datasets under a separate rights, hosting,
  integrity, and citation review.
- Reconsider deferred major capabilities only after the foundation is stable
  and through a separately approved design.
- Add platforms or major capabilities only after the foundation is stable and
  a concrete research need justifies them.
